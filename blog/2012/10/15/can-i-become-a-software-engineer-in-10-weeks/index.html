Can I Become a Software Engineer in 10 Weeks?

Oct 15th, 2012

My experiences as a student at Hackbright Academy began on October 1st, 2012. For those unfamiliar with Hackbright Academy, it is a 10-week intensive program that helps women with the problem solving mindset but without the traditional computer science background become software engineers and web developers. It is currently run by three amazing people: Christian, Charles and David (@chriszf, @cruhland, and @davjphillips, respectively).

I am one of the 16 women in the second batch, each of whom have distinctive backgrounds and skills. One thing everybody shares is passion and curiosity for learning to program, and discipline and endurance to pull through the learning challenges that will come up.

This blog will mostly be a technical blog, tracing my learning escapades during my time at Hackbright Academy. It will focus on my codes, endeavours to debug and refactor them, and personal thoughts on both coding and non-coding alike. Any comments or feedback are always welcome.

It’s important to mention that Natasha’s blog was a great motivation for this blog. I find it inspiring that you can be a learner and a teacher at the same time, empowering other beginner coders by sharing learning experiences and resources. In my blog, I hope to do my best to keep a fresh learner’s perspective to help other self-learners out there, as Natasha and few others have done for me.

Another motivation I have for writing my blog is to capture my inspirations. Inspirational moments seems to come and go, and while I consider inspiration to be powerful only when accompanied by hard work and action, I hope to capture some inspirations through my blog as I transition from a thinker and an idealist to a builder and an engineer.

And here’s my present answer to the above question. Can I become a software engineer? Yes–only if I make it happen.
